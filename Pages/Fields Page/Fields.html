Fields

This interactive field encompasses an array of fields, each specializing in addressing unique challenges in healthcare through technology, biology, and engineering. By combining innovative technology with an understanding of the human body, biomedical engineers develop solutions ranging from medical devices and imaging systems to tissue engineering and computational models that advance diagnosis, treatment, and patient care.


Curious about specifics of any of these fields? Check out more information at Resources.




Biomechanics

Biomechanics is the study of how the human body moves and responds to forces. By applying a combination of physics and engineering, this branch of biomedical engineering helps us understand motion, improve athletic performance, prevent injuries, and design prosthetics.

Image Source: World Council of Biomechanics

Bioimaging

Bioimaging is a area of biomedical engineering that develops techniques to visualize the body and its functions. It includes technologies like MRI, CT scans, and ultrasound, helping diagonse, monitor, and study diseases.

Image Source: BioImage Archive accession SBSST584

Biomaterials

Biomaterials is a specialized field within biomedical engineering that focuses on studying, designing, and testing materials that can safely interact with the human body. Scientists in this branch help create implants, prosthetics, and tissue scaffolds to improve health and healing.

Image Source: Biolin Scientific

Bioinstrumation

Bioinstrumation is the process of designing and developing devices to measure, monitor, and analyze biological systems. This includes tools such as sensors, monitors, and diagonistic instruments that improve patient care and medical research.

Image Source: Freepik

Medical Imaging

Medical imaging is a specialized area within biomedical engineering that focuses particularly on developing and refining technologies that help doctors visualize the inside of the body for diagonsis and treatment. With its specific tools, it helps to enable accurate detection of diseases and works to guide medical decisions.

Image Source: Marque Urgent Care

Tissue Engineering

Tissue engineering is a branch of biomedical engineering that crafts biological substitutes to repair or replace damaged tissues and organs. It combines cells, biomaterials, and engineering techniques to develop functional tissues that support healing and restore body function.

Image Source: Celeste Nelson & Micheal Palmer

Clinical Engineering

Clinical engineering applies technology directly in healthcare settings through managing, maintaining, and improving medical equipment and systems to ensure patient safety. This area of expertise also strives to support effective treatment for all patients.

Image Source: University of Ottawa

Bioinformatics

Bioinformatics is the intergration of computers and data analysis in biology. It helps researchers to interpret genetic, molecular, and clinical data to advance medicine, drug development, and personalized healthcare.

Image Source: The Wistar Institute

Pharmaceutical Engineering

Pharmaceutical Engineering is a branch of biomedical engineering which specializes in designing and improving processes to develop & manufacture safe and effective drugs. It combines chemistry, biology, and engineering principles to ensure high-quality and efficiently produced medicines.

Image Source: ReAgent
