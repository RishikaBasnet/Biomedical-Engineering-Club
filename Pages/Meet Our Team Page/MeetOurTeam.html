Meet Our Team

The Biomedical Engineering Club is a group of curious and driven high school students passionate about educating students on the usage of technology in medicine. Beyond collaborating in the club, we each contribute to our community in our own unique ways.






President

Rishika Basnet

Rishika is currently a high school junior, passionate about pursuing cybersecurity and gaining more knowledge of computer science in the future. She helped launch the Biomedical Engineering Club to explore paths in computer science and its specific applications in the medical field. In her spare time, she volunteers as a museum docent at The Lawrence Hall of Science, holds a job as a math tutor in a local learning center, and serves as a member of the Stanford Neuroscience Journal Club.






Vice President

Grace Chen

Grace is currently a junior in high school, eager to pursue computational biology in her future. She joined the club because she feels that programming is increasingly relevant in various fields of science today and believes this integration of technology and medicine is particularly interesting. She enjoys gaining deeper insight on the medical world and how it ties back to computer programming, as well as sharing this knowledge. In her spare time, Grace enjoys programming games and drawing.
